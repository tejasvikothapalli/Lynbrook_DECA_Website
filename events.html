Event Selection

In DECA, there are two main types of events: roleplays and written events. Roleplays are similar to interviews or business consultations, where members are the interviewee or the business professional, and the judge is either interviewing members or seeking their help. On the other hand, written events are pre-prepared written components and presentations in which members must create an advertising plan, come up with a new business idea, or think of ways to improve existing businesses (there are more variations to this depending on the event selected).

One of the most important moments in DECA is choosing the right events. Members are allowed to compete at conferences with ONE written event and ONE roleplay event. We highly recommend members to participate in one of each type to maximize their chances of placing at a conference. Additionally, members may take part in as many online events and video challenges as they would like to potentially win any extra awards.

Roleplay Events

In roleplays, a situation will be presented to members when they arrive at their event, and they will have a short amount of time to prepare their response based on a given prompt and a set of indicators. The type of prompt and the content involved will defer based on its type of roleplay as well as the specific event. After this, members must go and present what they have come up with to the judge for approximately 10 minutes and answer any questions the judge may pose. When competing in roleplays, members must also take a test that will count for approximately 40% of the overall score.

There are three main types of roleplay events:

  • Principles Events
  • Individual Series Events
  • Team Decision Making Events

Principles Events

DECA's Principles Events measure members' proficiency in basic business concepts within all aspects of business, such as marketing, finance, business administration, emotional intelligence, etc. All members are tested through an interview situation with 10 minutes of preparation time, where members are interviewing for a position at the judge's company. Furthermore, these events are only open for first-year DECA members, so Lynbrook DECA recommends that all first-year members participate in a Principles Event for their roleplay, as they will then be competing against others with a similar level of experience.

Team Decision Making Events

DECA's TDM Events test members on both conceptual knowledge as well as team dynamics. All teams are tested through some form of corporate interaction, where teams are given a prompt with 30 minutes of preparation time and asked for their opinion as per what to do based on the given situation. The judge is their client who evaluates them based on content presented, style and clarity of presentation, as well as team dynamics between the partners. Members must also take an exam (depends on the event selected), and the average of both partners' scores will be taken as a team exam score.

Individual Series Events

DECA's Individual Series Events test members on both conceptual knowledge as well as thinking quickly under a time constraint. Members are given a prompt with 10 minutes of preparation time and asked for their opinion as per what to do based on the given situation. The judge is their client/boss who evaluates them based on content presented and style/clarity of presentation. Members must also take an exam (depends on the event selected), the score of which count towards 40% of the overall event score.
