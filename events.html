Event Selection

In DECA, there are two main types of events: roleplays and written events. Roleplays are similar to interviews or business consultations, where you are the interviewee or the business professional, and the judge is either interviewing you or seeking your help. On the other hand, written events are pre-prepared written components and presentations where you must create an advertising plan, come up with a new business idea, or think of ways to improve existing businesses.

In DECA, the most important part is choosing the right events. Members are allowed to compete at conferences with ONE written event and ONE roleplay. We highly recommend that each member participates one of each type to maximize the amount they learn and their chances of placing at a conference. Additionally, members may take part in as many online events and video challenges as they would like.

Roleplay Events

In roleplays, a situation will be presented to you when you walk in, and you will have a short amount of time to prepare your response based on a given prompt and a set of indicators. After this, you will have to go and present to the judge for approximately ten minutes and answer any questions he/she has. When competing in roleplays, you must also take a test that will count for approximately 40% of your overall score.

Principles Events

DECA's Principles Events measure a student's proficiency in basic business concepts in all aspects of business, such as marketing, finance, business administration, emotional intelligence, etc. Furthermore, these events are only open for first-year DECA members, so Lynbrook DECA recommends these events to all first-year members, as they will be competing against others with a similar level of experience.

All principles events are interview situations, where you are interviewing for a position at the judge's company. The content asked from each event is different (Principles of Marketing focuses more on marketing whereas Principles of Finance focuses more on finance), but general business concepts are tested no matter the event. Everyone who competes in a Principles Event is also required to take a Business Administration Core Exam no matter what specific event they are competing in.
