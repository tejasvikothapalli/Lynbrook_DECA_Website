Event Selection

In DECA, there are two main types of events: roleplays and written events. Roleplays are similar to interviews or business consultations, where members are the interviewee or the business professional, and the judge is either interviewing members or seeking their help. On the other hand, written events are pre-prepared written components and presentations in which members must create an advertising plan, come up with a new business idea, or think of ways to improve existing businesses (there are more variations to this depending on the event selected).

One of the most important moments in DECA is choosing the right events. Members are allowed to compete at conferences with ONE written event and ONE roleplay event. We highly recommend members to participate in one of each type to maximize their chances of placing at a conference. Additionally, members may take part in as many online events and video challenges as they would like to potentially win any extra awards.

Roleplay Events

In roleplays, a situation will be presented to members when they arrive at their event, and they will have a short amount of time to prepare their response based on a given prompt and a set of indicators. The type of prompt and the content involved will defer based on its type of roleplay as well as the specific event. After this, members must go and present what they have come up with to the judge for approximately 10 minutes and answer any questions the judge may pose. When competing in roleplays, members must also take a test that will count for approximately 40% of the overall score.

There are three types of roleplay events:

  • Principles Events
  • Individual Series Events
  • Team Decision Making Events

Principles Events

DECA's Principles Events measure members' proficiency in basic business concepts within all aspects of business, such as marketing, finance, business administration, emotional intelligence, etc. All members are tested through an interview situation with 10 minutes of preparation time, where members are interviewing for a position at the judge's company. Furthermore, these events are only open for first-year DECA members, so we highly recommend that all first-year members participate in a Principles Event for their roleplay, as they will then be competing against others with a similar level of experience.

Team Decision Making Events

DECA's TDM Events test members on both conceptual knowledge as well as team dynamics. All teams are tested through some form of corporate interaction, where teams are given a prompt with 30 minutes of preparation time and asked for their opinion as per what to do based on the given situation. The judge is their client who evaluates them based on content presented, style and clarity of presentation, as well as team dynamics between the partners. Members must also take an exam (depends on the event selected), and the average of both partners' scores will be taken as a team exam score.

Written Events

Written events are not as on-the-spot when compared to roleplays, because members must complete all aspects of the event before coming to the conference, including their written component, presentation, etc. Every written event is a bit different, but in general, each event has a written component as well as a presentation that must be made to the judge. The size of the written portion, the length of the presentation, and the task for the event is all dependent on what event members select.

There are five types of written events:

  • Business Operations Research Events
  • Chapter Team Events
  • Entrepreneurship Events
  • Marketing Representative Events
  • Professional Selling Events

Business Operations Research Events

DECA’s BOR Events provide opportunities for members to demonstrate knowledge and skills needed by management personnel. Every year, DECA introduces a new topic that tests each participant's ability to develop a detailed business plan that solves or helps solve the problem presented. Members chooose a company of their liking, and based on the given topic, they must research the company and present what they have found, as well as the changes they think should be implemented. In this year's prompt, members are required to analyze current employee engagement and then present a plan to improve employee engagement within their chosen business or organization. Each type of BOR event has the same prompt, and they only differ in what company you are able to select to write your plan on. And so, we highly recommend that members choose a company, see what event it fits under, and then select that event, rather than selecting an event and then picking the company. No exam needs to be taken for a BOR event.

There are some common traits across all BORs:

  • Participants: Team of 1-3
  • Page Count (Maximum): 30 Pages
  • Presentation Time: 15 Minutes

Chapter Team Events

CTEs provide opportunities for members to become more involved within the chapter by attempting to run a successful campaign and engage everyone within the Lynbrook DECA chapter to help out and support the campaign. Because of the level of involvement required by these events, we highly recommend that only experienced members consider selecting one of these events for their written event choice. No exam is required for participating in a CTE. Furthermore, each chapter is only allowed to send one team to compete for each of the CTEs, so if anyone would like to compete in one of them, please inform a Lynbrook DECA officer ASAP so we can discuss your project.

There are some common traits across all CTEs:

  • Participants: Team of 1-3
  • Page Count (Maximum): 30 Pages

Entrepreneurship Events

DECA's ENTREs provide opportunities for members to exlore the entrepreneurial process, from idea generation to business planning to implementation. By recognizing new innovative opportunities, participants are required to generate new ideas to take advantage of market opportunities to introduce a new business, product, or service. Through various different events, members get to explore how businesses are started, improved, and expanded. No exam needs to be taken for an ENTRE event.

There are some common traits across all ENTREs:

  • Presentation Time: 15 Minutes

Marketing Representative Events

MREs are marketing-related written events in which members exlore how to design a campaign/promotion for a company/group and will learn about marketing costs, how to implement advertising methods, and how to forecast generated revenue. Everyone competing in an MRE is required to take a Marketing Cluster Exam. If a member is in a team of more than one person, the average of the teams' scores are taken into account. Also, if a member is doing a roleplay with a marketing exam, then the exam will be taken once, and will be counted for both the written event and the roleplay event. If it's a roleplay with a different exam, then the member must take both exams.

There are some common traits across all ENTREs:

  • Page Count (Maximum): 11 Pages

Professional Selling Events

DECA’s PSEs provide opportunities for members to market a product/company to a prospective buyer or provide a client with advice. A PSE is oriented around a person's speaking skills and provided ideas, and so does not have a written component (Yes, it's a written event without a written portion). Therefore, a PSE only requires members to create a presentation that answers the prompt provided by DECA. The prompt is different for each event.

There are some common traits across all PSEs:

  • Page Count (Maximum): 0 Pages

Online Events

DECA’s Online Events provide opportunities for members to participate in online simulations to learn how to invest in the stock market, manage personal finances, or operate a business venture. Online events don't count as written events or roleplays, so members may participate in them outside of their regular events, and these online events serve as a way to place for ICDC without placing at conferences. Members may participate in as many online events as they'd like, but no more than one account for each.

There are two types of online events:

  • Stock Market Game
  • Virtual Business Challenges

Stock Market Game

Participants in the Stock Market Game develop and manage an investment portfolio. Each participating team manages all aspects of the portfolio including stock selection, buying and selling. The goal of the competition is to increase the value of the beginning portfolio. Members may compete in Teams of 1-3, and there is nothing else besides the trading in the online portfolio that has to be done (no written component, test, etc.). For Lynbrook, the top 25 teams in Western USA will advance to compete at ICDC. Winners will be judged on the growth of their portfolio from the initial amount of virtual money.

Virtual Business Challenges

Participants in the DECA Virtual Business Challenge (VBC) operate a computerized business simulation utilizing a competition version of the Virtual Business software. The VBC qualifying rounds are conducted via the Internet, where participants post competition scores for chartered association, regional and overall rankings. Think of VBC as a SIMS game, but oriented towards business and with profit as the main goal. Members can compete in VBCs as Teams of 1-3. The top two teams from each region (for Lynbrook, that's Western USA) will be able to compete at ICDC.

Event Description

Participants will analyze accounting documents such as T-accounts, worksheets, journals and the general ledger as well as source documents such as time sheets, customer invoices, pay records and more in order to identify issues.
VBC Hotel Management (VBCHM) In the VBC Hotel Management track, participants will manage various aspects of a hotel such as determining room rates, making bids on group sales and setting front desk and housekeeping staffing schedules. Participants will monitor their competitors, analyze various reports and financial information as well as keep a close watch on ratings and social media feedback.
VBC Personal Finance (VBCPF) In the VBC Personal Finance track, participants will manage bank accounts, credit and debit cards, bills, credit scores, taxes, insurance and investing. Participants will interpret actions, charts and graphs to make strategic financial decisions and optimize net worth.
VBC Restaurant (VBCRS) In the VBC Restaurant track, participants will manage specific marketing and business concepts such as market research, menu design and pricing, advertising strategies and purchasing within a restaurant. Participants will interpret actions, reports and financial information in order to make strategic marketing decisions for their restaurant to optimize profitability.
VBC Retail (VBCRT) In the VBC Retailing track, participants will manage specific marketing concepts such as pricing, purchasing and promotion within a convenience store. Participants will interpret actions, charts and graphs in order to make strategic marketing decisions for their store to optimize profitability.
VBC Accounting (VBCSP) In the VBC Sports track, participants will manage specific marketing concepts such as ticket pricing, media planning and sponsorships within a football franchise. Participants will interpret actions, charts and graphs in order to make strategic marketing decisions for their franchise to optimize profitability.

Video Challenges

DECA and several corporate partners collaborate to offer DECA members opportunities to demonstrate the knowledge and skills learned in the classroom through innovative challenges. Each challenge has a unique focus and a specific set of required tasks, but one common element is the requirement that presentations are made via YouTube videos. These video challenges are not considered competitive events, but taking part gives members a chance to win cash prizes, awards, recognition, and more! There are three video challenges, as listed below.

DECA Idea Challenge

The DECA Idea Challenge, a featured event of Global Entrepreneurship Week, is a fast-paced competition that challenges elementary, secondary and collegiate students around the globe to find an innovative new use for a common, everyday item in just eight days. Student teams of three to four members are asked to pitch their invention in a creative three-minute YouTube video presentation. The everyday item won't be announced until November 2015, which makes this Idea Challenge very challenging!

Finish Line Challenge

Participants in the Finish Line Challenge will assist Finish Line in analyzing its omni-channel strategy (providing a consistent and premium customer experience through the many ways in which the customer shops). This includes researching the customer experience through mobile technology, the Internet, social media, offline media, and in-store.

FIDM Challenge

Participants in the Fashion Institute of Design and Merchandising (FIDM) Challenge will translate a seasonal fashion trend into a “look” depicting the researched fashion forecast. First, participants will forecast a fashion trend for fall/autumn 2015. Second, participants will put together an outfit that your demographic would wear depicting this up and coming trend. Third, participants must explain why, based on their research, they believe in their trend forecast.
